# Diffusion and the 1/2 Scaling Signature

This page summarizes a conceptual framework for diagnosing diffusive spread from spatial scaling relationships.

## Why it matters here

Wildfire spread, plume behavior, ecological dispersal, and related spatial processes are often analyzed through scaling relationships. The `1/2` diffusion signature provides a useful baseline against which faster, slower, or more structured forms of spread can be compared.

## Core ideas

- normal diffusion produces `MSD ~ t`
- spatial spread then follows `distance ~ t^(1/2)`
- the `1/2` exponent is a diagnostic signature of diffusive transport
- departures from that signature can indicate subdiffusion, superdiffusion, or directed transport

## Relevance to proposal framing

- supports arguments about scaling analysis in wildfire or environmental spread processes
- provides a baseline theory for interpreting empirical spatiotemporal patterns
- helps distinguish random spread from structured transport mechanisms
