# Fire Modeling Frameworks

This page summarizes how major wildfire modeling frameworks look when viewed through a scaling and interface-growth lens.

## Why it matters here

Operational wildfire models are often excellent at local spread physics, but they typically say much less about emergent perimeter geometry, fractal roughness, or large-scale scaling exponents. That gap matters if the proposal wants to connect local dynamics to landscape-scale pattern.

## Core ideas

- rate-of-spread and Huygens-style models emphasize local front velocity
- elliptical growth models capture anisotropy but usually assume smooth geometry
- cellular automata and percolation models more naturally produce irregular and fractal patterns
- high-fidelity physics-based simulators still rarely analyze emergent perimeter scaling explicitly

## Relevance to proposal framing

- helps position the project as a bridge between local fire physics and emergent geometry
- clarifies where scaling diagnostics can complement existing modeling frameworks
- supports arguments for cross-scale wildfire modeling rather than purely local rate-of-spread prediction
