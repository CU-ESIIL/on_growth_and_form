# Superdiffusive Wildfire Perimeters

This page summarizes a conceptual framework in which wildfire perimeter growth may follow superdiffusive rather than classical diffusive scaling.

## Why it matters here

If wildfire perimeters grow closer to `P(t) ~ t^(2/3)` than to a classical diffusive `1/2` law, then wildfire spread may need to be interpreted as fractal interface growth through heterogeneous, connected landscapes rather than only as local combustion transport.

## Core ideas

- observed perimeter growth may approach `P(t) ~ t^(2/3)`
- fractal perimeter dimensions near `4/3` provide one geometric explanation
- percolation and connectivity may shape wildfire-front geometry
- spotting and long-range jumps can support superdiffusive spread

## Relevance to proposal framing

- supports cross-scale and geometry-aware wildfire modeling arguments
- links wildfire spread to percolation, criticality, and growing-interface theory
- suggests model structure should account for heterogeneity, connectivity, and nonlocal spread
