# Wildfire Scaling Research Agenda

This page frames the wildfire scaling problem as a set of open questions and empirical tests rather than as a settled result.

## Why it matters here

This is useful proposal material because it turns a broad idea about wildfire geometry into a concrete research agenda. Instead of only saying that scaling might matter, it spells out what must be measured, which mechanisms are plausible, and how `FIRED` plus `CubeDynamics` could make those tests possible.

## Core questions

- does a reproducible perimeter-scaling regime actually exist?
- when during a fire lifetime does scaling apply?
- what mechanisms produce the observed exponent?
- is fractal perimeter dimension universal or environment-dependent?
- do current fire models reproduce the same geometric behavior?

## Why this helps the proposal

- it converts background theory into testable research questions
- it links satellite observations directly to modeling evaluation
- it shows how the project can bridge fire behavior, scaling theory, and data infrastructure
- it gives reviewers a clear path from hypothesis to measurement
