## What this section is for

Theory is the conceptual spine of the site. It explains the project's core claim, shows how it connects to broader traditions in growth and form, and makes room for the historical story of what wildfire science has emphasized and what it has left underdeveloped.

## Start with these pages

- [On Growth and Form](on-growth-and-form.md) for the philosophical and conceptual framing.
- [Wildfire Scaling Hypothesis](wildfire-scaling-hypothesis.md) for the project's central scientific claim.
- [Scaling Regimes in Fire Growth](wildfire-scaling-regime-hypothesis.md) for the idea that different growth dynamics may dominate in different conditions.

## What you will find deeper in the section

- Mechanistic and diagnostic extensions, including [Diffusion and 1/2 Scaling](diffusion-half-scaling.md), [Non-1/2 Scaling Signatures](non-half-scaling-signatures.md), and [Wildfire Scaling and Dimensionality](wildfire-scaling-dimensionality.md).
- Spatial and perimeter-focused arguments such as [Scale-Conditioned WUI Geometry](scale-conditioned-wui-geometry.md) and [Superdiffusive Wildfire Perimeters](superdiffusive-wildfire-perimeters.md).
- The [Open Questions](wildfire-scaling-research-agenda.md) page, which turns the theory into an explicit research agenda rather than a closed doctrine.

## Why the historical arc matters

Readers who want to understand why this theory is being proposed now should pair this section with the [Causal History of Wildfire Modeling](../project-overview/causal-history-wildfire-modeling.md). The theory is positioned as an extension and reorganization of existing fire science, not as an abstract replacement for it.
