# Wildfire Scaling and Dimensionality

This page summarizes several scaling and geometric ideas used in theoretical wildfire research.

## Why it matters here

These frameworks provide a language for interpreting wildfire regimes not just as local rate-of-spread problems, but as multi-scale spatial systems with power-law event distributions, fractal perimeters, and connectivity-driven transitions.

## Core ideas

- fire sizes often follow power-law distributions
- fire perimeters can exhibit weak to moderate fractal structure
- burned area often scales roughly like a two-dimensional cluster
- percolation and self-organized criticality offer candidate explanations for scale-invariant wildfire behavior

## Relevance to proposal framing

- supports arguments for cross-scale wildfire modeling
- links wildfire research to broader statistical physics and complex systems theory
- provides a basis for thinking about connectivity, geometry, and scaling in model design
