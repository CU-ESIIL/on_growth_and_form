# Wildfire Scaling Regime Hypothesis

This page summarizes a proposal-shaping hypothesis: wildfire scaling may be regime-specific rather than valid across the entire fire lifetime.

## Why it matters here

This is one of the clearest ways to make the project ambitious without overclaiming. Instead of asserting a single universal wildfire scaling law from ignition to extinction, the hypothesis says that scaling may emerge only during a middle expansion phase and then break down again as the fire becomes constrained.

## Core idea

The working hypothesis is a three-phase fire lifetime:

1. ignition / nucleation
2. expansion / scaling
3. constraint / termination

Under this view, the strongest candidate for a reproducible scaling law is the expansion phase, where perimeter growth may approach `P(t) ~ t^(2/3)` and boundary geometry may approach a fractal dimension near `4/3`.

## Why this is useful for the proposal

- it turns a possible reviewer objection into a testable claim
- it avoids overextending a scaling law across obviously different fire stages
- it creates a clean empirical strategy using breakpoints, segmented fits, and scaling collapse
- it gives wildfire models a new benchmark: not just local spread, but the timing and geometry of the scaling regime
