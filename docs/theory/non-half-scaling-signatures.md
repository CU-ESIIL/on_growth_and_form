# Non-1/2 Scaling Signatures

This page summarizes anomalous transport regimes in which spatial spread deviates from the classical diffusive `1/2` signature.

## Why it matters here

Wildfire spread, dispersal, plume dynamics, and other environmental processes may not behave like normal diffusion. Looking for non-`1/2` scaling helps distinguish constrained, turbulent, directed, or long-jump transport from a simple random-walk baseline.

## Core ideas

- classical diffusion gives `distance ~ t^(1/2)`
- anomalous transport can be represented as `MSD ~ t^α`
- `α < 1` indicates subdiffusion
- `α > 1` indicates superdiffusion
- `α = 2` corresponds to ballistic motion

## Relevance to proposal framing

- supports arguments about different spread regimes in wildfire and environmental systems
- provides a language for diagnosing transport mechanisms from spatiotemporal data
- complements the `1/2` diffusion baseline with a broader anomalous-transport framework
